# Hackherthon_Proj_02
## Description

The climate analysis for Delhi, a region characterized by its extreme temperatures and varying precipitation, reveals significant insights into its changing climate. Historically, Delhi experiences a hot semi-arid climate, with scorching summers, a monsoon season bringing substantial rainfall, and mild to chilly winters. Over recent decades, data shows a gradual increase in average temperatures, pointing towards a warming trend. Seasonal analysis highlights shifting patterns, such as shorter winters and prolonged summers, alongside an alteration in the timing and intensity of the monsoon rains.This time series analysis is crucial for understanding the impacts of global warming on a micro scale and for developing local strategies to combat climate change effect
